Abdul Ali (Player 199)

Abdul Ali, known as Player 199, is one of the most sincere and beloved characters in Squid Game. A Pakistani immigrant working in South Korea, Ali represents the voice of trust, loyalty, and honesty in a game that tests human morality to its limit.

Background and Personality

Ali is a migrant worker who came to South Korea in search of better opportunities for his family. Despite working hard in a factory, he is cheated by his employer and left without wages. Struggling to support his wife and child, he joins the Squid Game with the hope of changing their lives. Ali is humble, gentle, and unfailingly polite — often addressing others with respect and kindness, even in the harshest situations.

Appearance and Demeanor

Ali stands out with his warm smile and towering physical presence. He has a strong build from manual labor and is often seen with curious and trusting eyes. His soft-spoken nature and foreign accent emphasize both his outsider status and innocence. Even in the games, his sense of fairness and gratitude shines brightly.

Role in the Game

As Player 199, Ali is introduced as a kind-hearted and helpful participant who quickly forms a bond with Seong Gi-hun. In several key moments, Ali's strength and loyalty save his teammates. Tragically, his unwavering trust is manipulated, most notably by Cho Sang-woo during the marble game, which becomes one of the series’ most heartbreaking scenes. His elimination deeply affected viewers, making his arc one of the most emotionally impactful in the series.

Strengths and Skills

  • Physically strong and resilient
  • Loyal and emotionally supportive
  • Quick-thinking in critical moments
  • Upholds strong moral values under pressure

Impact on the Story

Abdul Ali serves as the emotional anchor in Squid Game, highlighting the importance of compassion and humanity. His character reminds the audience of the tragic cost of betrayal and how innocence can be crushed in a corrupt system. Ali’s death marks a shift in the tone of the series, pushing both the characters and viewers into a darker, more ruthless reality. He remains one of the most loved and mourned figures in the entire show.
